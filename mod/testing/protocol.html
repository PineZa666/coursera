Usability Testing Protocol — Balancr

Preparation & Setup

  • Devices: one smartphone and one laptop with Chrome Device Mode.
  • Prototype URL: provide deployed link to the 6‑page prototype.
  • Materials: consent form, note sheet, camera/phone for photos, post‑test questionnaire.
  • Roles: Moderator (facilitates, reads script), Note‑taker (observes, timestamps), Photographer (over‑the‑shoulder shots).

Informed Consent

  • Provide consent form and read summary aloud. Ensure voluntary participation and anonymity.
  • Collect name/signature and allow questions before starting.

Execution Steps & Roles

  • Moderator: greets participant, explains purpose (time‑balance tracking), reads instructions verbatim.
  • Participant: performs tasks without guidance beyond the script.
  • Note‑taker: records observations, quotes, navigation paths, and time to completion.

Script (Read to Participant)

  • “You’ll use a mobile prototype to log activities and view your balance. Think aloud as you go.”
  • Task 1: “Create an account.”
  • Task 2: “Add three activities across different categories.”
  • Task 3: “Open the dashboard and describe the balance you see.”
  • Task 4: “Remove one activity and add a new one to improve balance.”
  • Exploration: “Spend 1–2 minutes exploring anything else you find.”

Materials

  • Post‑test questionnaire: SUS‑style satisfaction (1–5), clarity of navigation, perceived usefulness, suggestions.
  • Interview prompts: “What felt easy/hard?”, “What would you change?”, “Where did you hesitate?”

Recording Observations

  • Timestamp key events (start, submit, page switches); record quotes verbatim.
  • Capture photos with captions showing breakdowns/opportunities (over‑the‑shoulder and device context).
  • Log interaction events locally (A/B pages record view, preset, submit).

Debrief

  • Thank participant; explain purpose of A/B redesign; confirm anonymity.
  • Ask for any final suggestions; obtain permission to use photos in the report.
